A pepeha is a traditional Māori introduction that establishes identity by referring to the ancestral landmarks, iwi (tribe), hapū (sub-tribe), and personal connections to the land. It's a way of introducing oneself in a manner that connects to your place in the world. Historically, pepeha has been a significant part of Māori culture, serving not only as an introduction but also as a reminder of one's whakapapa (genealogy) and tūrangawaewae (place of standing).

In Māori culture, a pepeha is more than just a way to introduce yourself; it is a connection to the land, the mountains, the rivers, and the ancestors who have come before. It is a reflection of identity and belonging, reinforcing the deep relationship between people and their environment. Understanding and sharing pepeha is crucial to preserving and passing on cultural knowledge and values.
